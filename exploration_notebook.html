Introduction¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area.

Bay Wheels is a regional public bicycle sharing system in the San Francisco Bay Area, California operated by Motivate in a partnership with the Metropolitan Transportation Commission and the Bay Area Air Quality Management District. Bay Wheels is the first regional and large-scale bicycle sharing system deployed in California and on the West Coast of the United States. It was established as Bay Area Bike Share in August 2013. As of January 2018, the Bay Wheels system had over 2,600 bicycles in 262 stations across San Francisco, East Bay and San Jose.

        print("Columns with Null/NAN Value\n")

Conclusion from above 3 Graphs:¶

  • The trip distribution over day hours peaks around two timeframes, 8 AM-9 AM and 4 PM-6 PM, during typical rush hours, where 5PM is the peak time.
  • While focusing on the trip distribution over day of week plot, the majority of rides happened on working days (Mon-Fri).
  • Deeply analysing the 3rd graph i.e. trip distribution over months I found that the trip distribution is extremely low during April and May, the possible reason could be the lockdown implemented due to Corona Virus Pandemic.

So finally I can conclude that the peoples opting for bikes, majority of them are working professionals because first two graphs show a standard timing of 8-9 AM and 5-6 PM and working days, which mostly prefered for working professionals. The third graph shows a sudden downfall in the month of April and March, and the possible reason is the Lockdown due to impact of Corona Virus.
